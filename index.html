    What are SLOs?

    Service Level Objectives

    A Service Level Indicator (SLI) measures a specific aspect of your service ‚Äî like request success rate or response time. A Service Level Objective (SLO) sets the target for that measure ‚Äî for example, "99.9% of requests succeed". Together they define what good looks like for your users.

    Why SLOs Matter

    SLOs help teams balance reliability with innovation, make data-driven decisions, and establish clear expectations with stakeholders about service quality.

    Key Benefits

    Better incident response, improved prioritization, clearer communication with customers, and a shared understanding of acceptable reliability levels.

    SLO Examples in Practice

    Here are three common SLOs used in production services. Each example shows the SLI being measured, the target, and what it means for users.

    Availability
    99.9%
    SLI
    Percentage of HTTP requests that return a successful response (2xx/3xx)
    Target
    99.9% of requests succeed over a 30-day rolling window
    Means
    At most ~43 minutes of downtime per month is acceptable
    Latency
    < 200ms
    SLI
    Distribution of HTTP request durations, measured at the 95th percentile
    Target
    p95 latency < 200ms, measured over a 1-hour window
    Means
    95% of users get a response in under 200ms ‚Äî only 1 in 20 requests may be slower
    Error Rate
    < 0.1%
    SLI
    Ratio of failed requests (5xx responses) to total requests
    Target
    Error rate stays below 0.1% per day
    Means
    No more than 1 error per 1,000 requests ‚Äî leaving a small, predictable error budget
